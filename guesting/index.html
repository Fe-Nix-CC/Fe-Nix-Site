How To Guest

About Guesting

In Old School RuneScape, players are allowed to guest in at most one clan. This allows them to chat in two clans at a time, the one they are a member of, and the one they are guesting in. In Fe Nix, guests are allowed to view and participate in the chat, but may not be able to see announcements due to limitations of the system. We also allow clan members to guest on accounts other than ones that are members.

The rules for guesting in Fe Nix are the same as for members of the clan, and you can read those here. Note that guests must be free to play while guesting.

How to Guest

To guest in a clan, you must first open the grouping tab. This is usually found in the bottom right of the screen, and its appearance depends on your UI settings.

The button to navigate to the grouping tab is in a different position on different layouts, and its icon may be different depending what it was last used for.

Once you have opened the grouping tab, it should look like this, though which sub-tab is selected might vary, as well as how many sub-tabs there are.

Now you must open the guesting tab.

Once that is open, the tab should read "View another clan" if you aren't currently guesting in a clan. To guest in a clan, you must press the find button.

That will open a prompt in the chat box, in the bottom left, where you must type in the name of the clan, "Fe Nix". Capitalisation doesn't matter.


If that has worked, the guesting sub-tab should now say "Fe Nix" at the top.

To join the chat channel, you must click the "chat" button.

Once chatting in the chat channel, messages can be sent there by either starting the message with three slashes "///", or starting the message with "/gc", or you can send the message "/@gc" to send messages without a slash at the start to group chat.

If you are guesting in a clan, you may leave at any time by pressing the "discard" button in the guesting sub-tab.
